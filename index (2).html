Project PHYTOLEARN

Welcome to PHYTOLEARN, a website created to help people learn more about urban gardening and the importance of plants in our daily lives. This platform aims to raise awareness about plant blindness and encourage individuals to appreciate the role of plants in the environment.

PURPOSE

One of the main challenges in urban planting is the lack of knowledge among individuals on how to properly grow vegetables and fruits, as well as understand their nutritional benefits. Many people are interested in planting but struggle due to limited access to clear and reliable information. This issue is strongly connected to plant blindness, where individuals underestimate the importance of plants and lack the necessary knowledge to engage in planting activities.

To address these gaps, the proposed website will provide simple, clear, and scientifically grounded information about planting. Its goal is to transform complex botanical concepts into easy-to-understand and accessible content. By offering a structured learning approach, the platform aligns with the findings of Pongsophon and Jituafua (2021), which suggest that guided learning significantly improves botanical literacy, or a person’s ability to understand and grow plants.

In many urban areas, people tend to prioritize infrastructure such as buildings and roads, often overlooking the environmental value of plants. This project emphasizes that plants are not only a source of food but also play a vital role in absorbing carbon dioxide, improving air quality, and reducing temperature in urban environments. As highlighted by Gavia et al. (2021), gardening also provides holistic and therapeutic benefits that contribute to improved well-being and stress management.

DESCRIPTION

This proposed website is a digital, web-based guide designed to support urban planting within city environments. It provides comprehensive information about vegetables, fruits, flowers, and trees that can be grown in limited spaces such as balconies, rooftops, and small backyards. The platform includes essential details about plant nutrients and their contributions to human health, along with practical guidance on proper watering, sunlight exposure, and soil preparation to ensure healthy plant growth.

The website offers step-by-step instructions on how to plant, care for, and harvest various crops. It also presents basic scientific concepts related to plant nutrition and explains the environmental benefits of planting, such as improving air quality and maintaining ecological balance. To enhance user engagement, the platform incorporates video tutorials and specialized guides, including techniques such as hydroponics, where plants are grown in nutrient-rich water without soil.

Furthermore, the website provides location-based tips tailored to users’ environment and seasonal conditions, making planting more accessible, engaging, and effective through the use of digital technology.

INTRODUCTION

What is PHYTOLEARN?

The project “PHYTOLEARN” aims to develop a user-friendly, mobile-based guide that enables urban residents to cultivate their own food even within limited spaces. It seeks to deepen understanding of plant nutrition while promoting environmentally sustainable practices through the integration of modern science and digital technology. The platform also envisions creating a community where individuals become more aware of the importance of plants and are encouraged to grow their own food despite space limitations. Through the use of technology, the project aims to inspire individuals to engage in sustainable planting practices and increase plant awareness, ultimately contributing to a healthier environment for future generations.

According to Wandersee and Schussler (2001), “plant blindness” refers to the human tendency to overlook the importance of plants in the environment. This concept explains why many people fail to recognize the critical role of plants in maintaining ecological balance. Supporting this idea, Parsley (2020) stated that plant blindness has been a long-standing issue affecting botany education and awareness, where plants are often placed in the background compared to other living organisms. Similarly, Achurra (2022) noted that plants are less noticeable because they are static and lack visible features such as faces, resulting in reduced interest and botanical illiteracy.

In the local context, Gonzales et al. (2026) found that plant awareness among Filipino students remains limited, contributing to gaps in environmental engagement and conservation. Meanwhile, urban communities in Metro Manila have implemented urban agriculture initiatives, such as community gardens supported by local authorities, to address challenges including limited food access, environmental sustainability, and lack of green spaces (Montefrio & Saguin, 2025). These findings highlight the need to promote plant awareness and education, especially in urban settings.
